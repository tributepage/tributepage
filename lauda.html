Niki Lauda

Andreas Nikolaus "Niki" Lauda was born 22nd February 1949 in Vienna, Austria to a wealthy industrialist family. Is an Formula One driver and a three-time F1 World Drivers' Champion, winning in 1975, 1977 and 1984.

After a serious accident at the Nurburgring, where his car flew off the track, hit the barrage and caught fire, and Lauda's life was in grave danger, Niki only six weeks back in the race.

He is currently the only driver to have been champion for both Ferrari and McLaren, the sport's two most successful constructors. Currently working as a pundit for German TV during Grand Prix weekends and acts as non-executive chairman of the Mercedes AMG Petronas F1 Team.

See below the most important years in the racing career:

1971

Debut

1971 close

Nicky's family did not share his enthusiasm for motor sport and refused to give him financial support. Realizing that something needs to be changed dramatically, Lauda took a big loan from the bank and was able to buy himself a place in the March team, which played in Formula 2, for the 1971 season.

After successful performances in Formula-2, Nicky's talent was noticed by the owner of the team Robin Hurd, and Lauda was invited to perform also in Formula-1 (the team played in two series at once).

1974

Ferrary

1974 close

Lauda's talent came true only when his team-mate Clay Regazzoni moved to the Ferrari team in 1974, and its owner, Enzo Ferrari, asked him what he thought of Niki Lauda. Regazzoni spoke positively, and Ferrari signed a contract with Lauda for the 1974 season.

The confidence that the team showed to the not-so-famous Niki Lauda became quickly justified - already in his debut race for Ferrari, Argentina's Grand Prix, Lauda took second place. The first victory in Formula-1, which was also the first for Ferrari since 1972, was won by him only three races, at the Spanish Grand Prix. However, although Lauda attracted universal attention in the 1974 season, winning in particular eight poles (six of them in a row), lack of experience and insufficient reliability of Ferrari cars led to the fact that he was able to win only once, at the Grand Prix Netherlands, and by the end of the season he took the fourth place in the championship.

1975

First championship

1975 close

The season of 1975 began for Lauda not very well - in four races he could not rise above the fifth place. But then, starting to perform on a new Ferrari 312T car, he won four races from the next five. After winning the last race of the season, the US Grand Prix, Nicky Lauda for the first time became world champion.

1976

Crash in Nurburgring

1976 close

In the 1976 season, Lauda, ​​on the contrary, immediately took over the lead - in the first six races he won four wins and twice took the second place. When Nicky won the British Grand Prix, he was more than twice ahead of Jodie Scheckter's nearest opponent, and it seemed that nothing could prevent him from winning the second champion title.

But in the next race, the German Grand Prix at the Nurburgring, Niki Lauda was in a serious accident.

Lauda was seriously injured. His Ferrari broke out, and he was close to death after inhaling hot toxic fumes and severe burns. Nevertheless, he survived and was sufficiently restored to participate again in the Italian Grand Prix.

1977

Second championship

1977 close

The season of 1977 turned out to be difficult for Lauda, ​​although it was not difficult for him to win the championship - he won no big wins, but he performed very stably.

In the middle of the season, he announced his intention to leave Ferrari after the end of the season, but did it even earlier, after Ferrari announced on the third car of the unknown then Gilles Villeneuve at the Canadian Grand Prix.

In the last two races of the season Nicky Lauda did not participate, but despite this, he scored enough points to become the second time champion.

1979

Leave Formula 1

1979 close

In the seasons of 1978 and 1979, Nicky Lauda played for the Brabham team, owned at the time by Bernie Ecclestone. He did not manage to achieve significant results in these seasons.

The 1979 season turned out to be completely disastrous because of the constant technical problems that haunted the team, and Lauda was able to score just 4 points in the championship.

Before the 1979 Canadian Grand Prix Lauda announced the end of his career as a rider.

1982

Comeback. McLaren

1982 close

In the winter of 1982, Niki Lauda performed several successful tests for McLaren. The main sponsor of the team, the Marlboro brand, also supported his candidacy, and McLaren signed a contract with Lauda.

In the first race of the season the Austrian took the fourth place, and in the third race, the Western Grand Prix of the USA in Long Beach, won. But in the future the season was unsuccessful - Lauda could not complete five races because of technical problems, and one more was disqualified.

At the end of the season, he took only the fifth place in the championship with 30 points.

1984

Third title

1984 close

The season of 1984 began controversially - in the first eight races Lauda won two wins and once took the second place, but the remaining five races could not finish because of problems with the car. But the team McLaren managed to eliminate the shortcomings of the car by the middle of the season, and Lauda could perform much more stable.

He scored three more wins and three times took second place, and eventually won the world championship for the third time in his career.

1985

End of career

1985 close

However, the 1985 season was again marred by numerous technical problems, and most of the racing Nicky could not finish. He won only one of the races, and again took only tenth place in the championship.

After that, he announced the second time the completion of the career of the rider, this time finally.
